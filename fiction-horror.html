Carmilla
by Sheridan Le Fanu

Plot-Synopsis

"Carmilla" tells the story of Laura, a lonely young woman who encounters a mysterious woman named Carmilla. As their friendship develops, Laura begins to experience strange dreams and weakens physically. She soon discovers that Carmilla is actually a seductive vampire who feeds on her blood. With the help of General Spielsdorf and Doctor Hesselius, Laura tries to combat the evil that haunts her.

Frankenstein
by Mary Shelley

Plot-Synopsis

A young scientist, Victor Frankenstein, creates a grotesque but sentient creature in an unorthodox scientific experiment. Rejected by society and its creator, the creature seeks revenge, leading to tragic consequences for all involved.

The Phantom of the Opera
by Gaston Leroux

Plot-Synopsis

In the Paris Opera House, a mysterious masked figure known as the Phantom falls in love with Christine Daaé, a young soprano. As he mentors her, his obsession turns dangerous, leading to a dramatic and tragic conclusion.

The Castle of Otranto
by Horace Walpole

Plot-Synopsis

Considered the first gothic novel, "The Castle of Otranto" tells the tale of Manfred, the lord of the castle, whose family is plagued by an ancient prophecy. Filled with supernatural events, betrayal, and forbidden love, it sets the stage for gothic fiction.
