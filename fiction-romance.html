Wuthering Heights
by Emily Brontë

Plot-Synopsis

Wuthering Heights is a story of love and revenge that follows
the life of Heathcliff, a young adopted man who suffers
rejection and cruelty despite the deep love of his adopted
sister, Catherine. The plot unfolds in a stormy environment,
marked by passion, envy, and hatred, reflecting the tormented
psychology of the protagonists.

Persuasion
by Jane Austen

Plot-Synopsis

Persuasion is the story of Anne Elliot, a sensitive and reserved
woman, who reunites with a love from the past, Captain
Frederick Wentworth. Through the plot, the novel explores
themes of regret, second chances in love, and the importance
of persuasion in personal decisions and social relationships.

Pride and Prejudice
by Jane Austen

Plot-Synopsis

Pride and Prejudice follows the story of Elizabeth Bennet and
her relationship with the haughty Mr. Darcy in 19th century
England. The plot focuses on social prejudices, love, and the
personal development of the characters in a historical context.

Jane Eyre
by Charlotte Bronte

Plot-Synopsis

Jane Eyre tells the story of Jane, a young orphan who becomes
a governess at Thornfield, where she meets Mr. Rochester.
The plot unfolds between mysteries and strange events, as
love blossoms between the protagonists, exploring Jane's
emotional development through life's adversities.

Emma
by Jane Austen

Plot-Synopsis

Emma tells the story of Emma Woodhouse, a young woman with
beauty, intelligence, and a privileged economic position.
Her misperceptions about love endanger her own happiness
and that of those around her.
